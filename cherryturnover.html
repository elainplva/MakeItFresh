Cherry Turnover

This homemade crumbly and butter cherry turnover is filled with fresh cherries and will become your new favourite dessert.


Cook Time: 40 minutes

Top Tips

  • You can use store-bought puff pastry for convenience, or you can make your own puff pastry from scratch if you prefer.
  • Feel free to add a sprinkle of cinnamon or lemon zest to the cherry filling for extra flavor.
  • Serve the turnovers as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a delicious dessert.

Equipment

  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper
  • Rolling pin
  • Pastry brush
  • Fork
  • Mixing bowl
  • Small saucepan
  • Spoon

Ingredients

    For the Dough:
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed (store-bought or homemade)
  • All-purpose flour (for dusting)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Granulated sugar (for sprinkling)
    • For Cherry Filling
  • 1 cup cherries, pitted and chopped (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the Cherry Filling:
    In a small saucepan, combine the chopped cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla extract. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and the cherries soften, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Roll Out the Puff Pastry:
    On a lightly floured surface, roll out the thawed puff pastry into a large square or rectangle, about 1/8 inch thick. Use a knife or pizza cutter to cut the pastry into equal-sized squares or rectangles, depending on your preference for turnover size.
  4. Fill the Turnovers:
    Place a spoonful of the cherry filling in the center of each pastry square. Be careful not to overfill.
  5. Fold and Seal the Turnovers:
    Fold each pastry square over the filling to create a triangle or rectangle shape, depending on the shape you cut. Use a fork to crimp the edges firmly to seal the turnovers.
  6. Brush with Egg Wash:
    Place the turnovers on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops of the turnovers with beaten egg using a pastry brush. This will give them a golden brown color when baked.
  7. Sprinkle with Sugar:
    Sprinkle the tops of the turnovers with a little granulated sugar for added sweetness and a crispy texture.
  8. Bake the Turnovers:
    Transfer the baking sheet to the preheated oven and bake the turnovers for 15-20 minutes, or until they are puffed and golden brown.
  9. Cool and Serve:
    Remove the turnovers from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve the cherry turnovers warm or at room temperature.
