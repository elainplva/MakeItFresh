Quiche Lorraine

This receipe be served as a breakfast, brunch, lunch, or even dinner dish, and it pairs well with a side salad or fresh fruit.


Cook Time: 45 minutes

Top Tips

  • For a shortcut, you can use a store-bought pie crust instead of making the crust from scratch.
  • Customize the quiche by adding other ingredients such as caramelized onions, mushrooms, spinach, or different types of cheese.
  • Make sure to let the quiche cool slightly before slicing to allow the filling to set and make clean slices.

Equipment

  • 9-inch pie dish or tart pan
  • Rolling pin (if making homemade crust)
  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk
  • Fork
  • Skillet
  • Baking sheet
  • Aluminum foil
  • Oven

Ingredients

    For the Crust:
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water
    • For the Filling:
  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or half-and-half)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust:
    In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use a fork or pastry cutter to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork until the dough comes together and forms a ball. Flatten the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Roll Out the Crust:
    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle large enough to fit into your pie dish or tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough to the dish and press it gently into the bottom and up the sides. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges as desired.
  3. Pre-bake the Crust:
    Line the crust with parchment paper or aluminum foil and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Remove the weights and parchment paper, then bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until the crust is lightly golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  4. Cook the Bacon:
    In a skillet over medium heat, cook the chopped bacon until crispy. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked bacon to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess grease.
  5. Assemble the Quiche:
    Sprinkle the cooked bacon and shredded Swiss cheese evenly over the pre-baked crust.
  6. Prepare the Egg Mixture:
    In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg (if using) until well combined.
  7. Pour Egg Mixture Over the Fillings:
    Pour the egg mixture evenly over the bacon and cheese in the crust.
  8. Bake the Quiche:
    Place the filled quiche on a baking sheet (to catch any potential spills) and bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.
