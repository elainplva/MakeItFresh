Pasta and Meatballs

This delcious receipe will make you feel like you are enjoying this dish in a famed italian restaraunt.


Cook Time: 60 minutes

Top Tips

  • Feel free to customize the meatballs by adding additional ingredients such as finely chopped onions, grated carrots, or herbs like basil or thyme.
  • You can make the meatballs ahead of time and freeze them for later use. Simply thaw them in the refrigerator overnight before cooking.
  • If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can puree the marinara sauce in a blender or food processor before heating it.

Equipment

  • Large pot for cooking pasta
  • Skillet or frying pan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Baking sheet (optional, for baking meatballs)
  • Wooden spoon or spatula
  • Slotted spoon
  • Colander
  • Serving dish

Ingredients

    For the Meatballs:
  • 1 pound ground beef (or a combination of beef and pork)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for frying)
    • For the Pasta:
  • 12 ounces (about 340g) of your favorite pasta (spaghetti, penne, etc.)
    • For the Sauce:
  • 2 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving
  • Chopped fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven (Optional):
    If baking the meatballs, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C)
  2. Cook Pasta:
    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Once cooked, drain the pasta in a colander and set aside.
  3. Prepare Meatball Mixture:
    In a mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, egg, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Use your hands or a wooden spoon to mix everything together until well combined.
  4. Form Meatballs:
    Roll the meat mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter, and place them on a plate or baking sheet.
  5. Cook Meatballs:
    Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil to the skillet. Once the oil is hot, add the meatballs in a single layer, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Cook the meatballs for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until browned and cooked through. You may need to work in batches depending on the size of your skillet.
  6. Bake Meatballs (Optional):
    Alternatively, you can bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes, or until cooked through.
  7. Warm Marinara Sauce:
    In a separate saucepan, heat the marinara sauce over medium heat until warmed through.
  8. Combine Pasta and Sauce:
    Once the pasta, meatballs, and marinara sauce are all cooked, add the pasta to the saucepan with the marinara sauce. Use tongs or a wooden spoon to toss the pasta until it is well coated with the sauce.
  9. Serve:
    Transfer the pasta and sauce to a serving dish. Arrange the cooked meatballs on top of the pasta. Sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh basil or parsley, if desired.
