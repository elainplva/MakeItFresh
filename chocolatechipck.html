Chocolate Chip Cookies

This homemade chocolate chip cookies receipe will leave wanting more with every bit of its chocolaty goodness


Cook Time: 20 minutes

Top Tips

  • For chewier cookies, slightly underbake them and let them cool completely on the baking sheet before removing.
  • You can customize these cookies by adding nuts (such as chopped walnuts or pecans) or different types of chocolate chips (such as milk chocolate or dark chocolate).
  • Store leftover cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, or freeze them for longer storage.

Equipment

  • Mixing bowls (2)
  • Whisk or electric mixer
  • Spatula
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper or silicone baking mat
  • Cookie scoop or spoon
  • Oven

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. Cream Butter and Sugars:
    In a mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. You can use a whisk or an electric mixer for this step.
  3. Add Eggs and Vanilla:
    Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then, mix in the vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. Combine Dry Ingredients:
    In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  5. Mix Wet and Dry Ingredients:
    Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a smooth dough forms. Be careful not to overmix.
  6. Add Chocolate Chips:
    Fold in the semisweet chocolate chips until evenly distributed throughout the cookie dough.
  7. Shape Cookies:
    Using a cookie scoop or spoon, scoop out portions of dough and place them onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each cookie for spreading.
  8. Bake the Cookies:
    Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden brown. The centers may still look slightly soft, but they will continue to set as they cool.
  9. Cool and Serve:
    Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, serve and enjoy your homemade chocolate chip cookies!
