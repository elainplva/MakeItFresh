Omellete

This homemade omellete receipe is so fluffy it melts in your mouth will go with anything for breakfast


Cook Time: 15 minutes

Top Tips

  • To prevent the omelette from sticking to the skillet, make sure to use enough cooking oil or butter, and ensure that the skillet is well-heated before adding the eggs.
  • For a lighter omelette, you can use egg whites instead of whole eggs or a combination of both.
  • Don't overcook the omelette; it should be slightly moist and creamy inside. If it's cooked for too long, it can become tough and rubbery.

Equipment

  • Mixing bowls
  • Non-stick skillet
  • Spatula or Whisk
  • Optional: Small plate or lid for covering the skillet

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fillings of your choice (e.g., cheese, vegetables, ham, bacon, herbs)
  • Cooking oil or butter for greasing the skillet

Instructions

  1. Prepare Fillings:
    If using any fillings such as vegetables or meats, chop them into small pieces or shreds and set them aside.
  2. Whisk Eggs:
    Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl. Add a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. Whisk the eggs vigorously until they are well beaten and frothy.
  3. Heat Skillet:
    Place a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add a small amount of cooking oil or butter and let it melt and coat the bottom of the skillet.
  4. Pour Eggs Into Skillet:
    Once the skillet is heated, pour the beaten eggs into the skillet. Use a spatula to gently spread the eggs evenly across the pan.
  5. Cook the Eggs:
    Let the eggs cook undisturbed for about 1-2 minutes, or until the edges start to set and lift away from the skillet.
  6. Add Fillings:
    Sprinkle your chosen fillings evenly over one half of the omelette. Be careful not to overfill, as it can make the omelette difficult to fold.
  7. Fold the Omelette:
    Using a spatula, carefully lift the unfilled half of the omelette and fold it over the filled half, creating a half-moon shape. Press down gently with the spatula to seal the edges.
  8. Finish Cooking:
    Continue to cook the omelette for another 1-2 minutes, or until the eggs are fully set and cooked through. You can cover the skillet with a lid or a small plate to help the top of the omelette cook faster.
  9. Slide Onto Plate:
    Once the omelette is cooked to your liking, carefully slide it out of the skillet onto a plate using the spatula.
