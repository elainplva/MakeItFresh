Oatmeal Pancakes

These homemade oatmeal pancakes are the perfect thing to have for a light but filling breakfast


Cook Time: 25 minutes

Top Tips

  • To make the pancakes gluten-free, you can use certified gluten-free oats and a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend.
  • If you prefer thinner pancakes, you can add a little more milk to the batter until you reach your desired consistency.
  • Leftover pancakes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for a few days or frozen for longer storage. Reheat them in the toaster or microwave before serving.

Equipment

  • Mixing bowls (2)
  • Whisk or fork
  • Non-stick skillet or griddle
  • Spatula
  • Measuring cups and spoons

Ingredients

  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted (or oil)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Oatmeal:
    In a mixing bowl, pour 1 cup of boiling water over the rolled oats. Let them soak for about 5 minutes while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients:
    In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined.
  3. Combine Wet Ingredients:
    In another bowl, beat the eggs and then mix in the milk, melted butter (or oil), and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Blend Oatmeal Mixture:
    Once the oats have soaked and softened, add them to the wet ingredients mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients:
    Gradually add the wet ingredients mixture to the dry ingredients mixture, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  6. Preheat Skillet or Griddle:
    Place a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter or oil.
  7. Cook Pancakes:
    Pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes.
  8. Flip and Cook:
    Carefully flip the pancakes with a spatula and cook for another 1-2 minutes on the other side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  9. Keep Warm:
    Transfer the cooked pancakes to a plate and cover them with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm while you cook the remaining pancakes. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
