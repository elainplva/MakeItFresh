Salmon

This is a simple yet elegant dinner will make your mouth water with the everlasting taste.


Cook Time: 20 minutes

Top Tips

  • Don't move the salmon around too much while it's cooking, as this can prevent it from developing a nice crust.
  • For extra flavor, you can marinate the salmon fillets in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and herbs for 30 minutes to an hour before cooking.
  • Leftover fried salmon can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat it gently in the microwave or oven before serving.

Equipment

  • Large skillet
  • Tongs or spatula
  • Paper towels
  • Plate

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets, skin-on or skinless, about 6-8 ounces each
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • Optional seasonings: lemon zest, garlic powder, paprika, herbs (such as dill or thyme)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Salmon:
    Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides of the salmon generously with salt and pepper. Optionally, you can also sprinkle on additional seasonings such as lemon zest, garlic powder, paprika, or herbs.
  2. Heat the Skillet:
    Heat the olive oil or vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until hot but not smoking. You want the oil to shimmer when you swirl the pan.
  3. Add the Salmon to the Skillet:
    Carefully place the seasoned salmon fillets in the hot skillet, skin-side down if using skin-on fillets. Make sure not to overcrowd the skillet; if necessary, cook the salmon in batches.
  4. Cook the Salmon:
    Let the salmon cook undisturbed for about 3-4 minutes, or until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Use tongs or a spatula to gently lift the edge of one fillet to check for doneness.
  5. Flip the Salmon:
    Once the bottom of the salmon is nicely browned, carefully flip the fillets over using tongs or a spatula. If cooking skin-on salmon, you may need to gently press down on the fillets to ensure even cooking.
  6. Continue Cooking:
    Cook the salmon on the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. The exact cooking time will depend on the thickness of the fillets.
  7. Remove from Heat:
    Once the salmon is cooked to your desired doneness, remove it from the skillet and transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  8. Serve:
    Serve the fried salmon immediately while it's still hot. You can garnish it with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon juice if desired.
