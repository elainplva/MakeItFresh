Chicken Salad

This salad is perfect for people who still want to eat delcious food and diet for the summer.


Cook Time: 30 minutes

Top Tips

  • Feel free to customize the salad with additional toppings such as avocado slices, crumbled feta or goat cheese, dried cranberries, or croutons.
  • You can cook the chicken breasts ahead of time and store them in the refrigerator until ready to use. This makes assembling the salad quicker and easier.
  • Adjust the dressing ingredients according to your taste preferences. If you prefer a lighter dressing, you can use less mayonnaise and more Greek yogurt or lemon juice.

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Cutting board
  • Knife
  • Medium saucepan or skillet
  • Tongs or spatula
  • Mixing spoon or fork

Ingredients

    For the Chicken:
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for cooking)
    • For the Salad:
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens (lettuce, spinach, arugula, etc.)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds or walnuts (optional)
    • For the Dressing:
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Greek yogurt (or sour cream)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Chicken:
    Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides. Heat a medium saucepan or skillet over medium-high heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Cook the chicken breasts for 6-8 minutes per side, or until cooked through and no longer pink in the center. Transfer the cooked chicken to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  2. Prepare the Salad Greens:
    Wash and dry the mixed salad greens thoroughly. Tear the larger leaves into bite-sized pieces if necessary and place them in a large mixing bowl.
  3. Add Toppings:
    Add the halved cherry tomatoes, sliced cucumber, thinly sliced red onion, and sliced almonds or walnuts (if using) to the bowl with the salad greens.
  4. Make the Dressing:
    In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Greek yogurt (or sour cream), lemon juice, Dijon mustard, minced garlic (if using), salt, and pepper until smooth and well combined.
  5. Slice the Chicken:
    Slice the cooked chicken breasts into thin strips or bite-sized pieces.
  6. Assemble the Salad:
    Add the sliced chicken to the bowl with the salad greens and toppings.
  7. Add Dressing:
    Drizzle the dressing over the salad ingredients in the bowl.
  8. Toss to Combine:
    Gently toss all of the salad ingredients together until evenly coated with the dressing.
  9. Serve:
    Divide the chicken salad among serving plates or bowls. You can serve it immediately, or refrigerate it for a while to chill before serving.
